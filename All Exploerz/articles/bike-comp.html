Factors That Affect Real-World Mileage

Manufacturer mileage claims are usually achieved under ideal testing conditions. Actual fuel economy depends on multiple factors including riding style, road conditions, maintenance, and traffic.

Riding Style

Aggressive acceleration and frequent hard braking significantly reduce fuel efficiency. Smooth throttle control generally produces better mileage.

Traffic Conditions

Heavy urban traffic can reduce mileage due to constant stopping and starting. Highway riding often delivers better fuel economy.

Maintenance

Regular servicing, proper tire pressure, clean air filters, and quality engine oil help maintain optimal efficiency.

Road Conditions

Steep climbs, rough roads, and carrying heavy loads require more engine power and therefore more fuel consumption.

Fuel Saving Tip: Keeping tire pressure at recommended levels can improve mileage while also extending tire life and improving safety.
